Interact Club 2018-2019

PRESENTING OF EYE - CAMP FUND OF RUPEES 15,000 TO ROTARY PRESIDENT BY INTERACT CLUB

Interact Club of SMCP Sanskar Vidya Bhavan attended Rotary Friendship Exchange (RFE) Programme on 29th January where the students got the opportunity to interact with the members of Rotarians from Taiwan. It was a great learning experience for the Interact Club members as they not only came to know about the Taiwanese culture but also got acquainted with the lifestyle of the people, their food, their festivals, etc. As a goodwill gesture friends from Taiwan presented each Interact Club member a Badge which is in the form of the map of Taiwan. On this occasion Shail Shah, the Interact President presented an amount of Rs. 15,000 to Mr. Shailesh Shah, the President of Rotary Club of Bharuch, Narmadanagari for the Eye - Checkup Camp which was organised in collaboration with Shankara Eye Hospital, Anand. The Students of SMCP Sanskar Vidya Bhavan held Exhibition Cum Sale of their Handicrafts and raised this fund. The members of Interact Club were appreciated by all the Rotarians for their dedicated service to the society.

EYE-CHECK UP CAMP BY INTERACT CLUB AT JAMBUSAR

The 200th Mega Eye Check Up Camp was sponsored by the Interact Cub of SMCP Sanskar Vidya Bhavan at Jambusar Navyug Government School on 5th January 2020. The Eye Camp was organised in association with Shankara Eye Hospital, Anand and was presided by Rtn.Parag Sheth, Rtn. Prashant Jani, Rtn. Shailesh Shah, many eminent Rotarians, Shankara Eye Hospital's Representative and many others dignitaries. The President of Ineract Club of SMCP Sanskar Vidya Bhavan Shail Shah and Secretary Kshitij Gurumoorthy and other students were felicitated by Rtn. Parag Sheth for raising the fund for the Eye Camp and their dedicated service to the Society. The students helped the Doctors and the other Hospital Authorities in the Eye Check up of the villagers.

CLOTH DISTRIBUTION IN SLUMS BY INTERACT AND EARLYACT CLUBS

The members of Interact and Earlyact Clubs strongly feel about the destitute and less previledged masses as for them nobody should suffer the lack of basic necessities like clothes. With this objective, Interact and Earlyact Club Members distributed Clothes and Woollens to the people living in Dubai - Tekri on 12th December 2019.

VISIT TO ANGANWADI BY INTERACT CLUB

In serving the best interest of the 'Community', the Interact Club of SMCP Sanskar Vidya Bhavan serves the best interest of the 'Humanity'. On 6th December' 2019, the members of Interact Club brought smiles on the innocent faces of the underprivileged kids of Anganwadi No. 4, Zadeshwar by distributing Toys and Woollen clothes to them. Rtn. Mr. Shailesh Shah, the President of Rotary Club of Bharuch, Narmada Nagari attended the event.

DISTRIBUTION OF TOYS AND WOOLLEN CLOTHES IN CIVIL HOSPITAL BY INTERACT CLUB

Interact Club of SMCP Sanskar Vidya Bhavan strives to bring smiles on the faces of the under privileged section of the society. With the same mission, the members of the Interact Club distributed Toys and Woollen clothes to the tiny toddlers in the Pediatric Ward of Civil Hospital on 4th December' 2019. The Parents of the Beneficiaries were emotionally touched by this gesture of the students and in turn blessed them.

KARATE TRAINING BY INTERACT CLUB AT KANYASHALA

nteract Club of SMCP Sanskar Vidya Bhavan organized Self Defense Training at Kanyashala on 18th November' 2019. This training aimed at empowering girls with Self-Defence techniques. The objectives of training session was to strengthen the self confidence and physical fitness of girl students. They were also trained how to tackle threat, overcome the fear factor and protect oneself with available tools.

SEWA BY INTERACT AND EARLYACT CLUBS AT GURUDWARA

To promote the spirit of dignity of labour , the members of Interact Club and Earlyact Clubs of SMCP Sanskar Vidya Bhavan zealously participated in the community service on the eve of the auspicious occasion of Guru Nanak Jayanti. On 11th November 2019 enthusiastic Interact and Earlyact members got a hands-on experience of doing various Community SEWA activities at Gurudwara which ranged from helping the kitchen staff in preparing chapatis, serving food to devotees, washing the utensils, arranging shoes on shelves etc.. The experience taught the students that “We have two hands - One to help ourselves and the second to help others. Without a sense of caring there can be no sense of community.”
